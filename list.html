The free list

The 12 grocery brands to never buy again

Each verdict below comes from reading the actual ingredient panel, not the front of the box. Next to every skip is the real swap. If we ever get one wrong, tell us and we will fix it publicly.

Print this list

Every verdict below comes from our published video investigations. Brands reformulate, so check the label if the recipe changes.

1. Breakstone's cottage cheese

Tapioca starch sits right after the milk and cream, doing the job cream should be doing.

Swap: Daisy. The whole list is cultured skim milk, cream, salt. No starch, no gums.

2. Sargento shredded cheese

Sells itself as 100% real natural cheese at a premium, but the bag still carries cellulose powder, potato starch and natamycin.

Swap: Tillamook. Farmer-owned since 1909, real aged cheese with only potato starch as an anti-caking agent.

3. Ball Park hot dogs

Mechanically separated chicken is ingredient number one. Pork is second.

Swap: Kirkland Signature. Beef, water, dextrose, salt, spices and curing agents. No byproducts, no fillers.

4. Oscar Mayer Deli Fresh smoked ham

Contains up to 24% seasoning solution, which is marketing speak for we added a lot of water.

Swap: Boar's Head Tavern Ham. The gold standard of the deli case since 1905.

5. Swanson beef broth

The front says no MSG added, but the footnote admits glutamate is in the yeast extract anyway.

Swap: Kitchen Basics Unsalted Beef Stock. The cleanest mainstream carton, starts with real beef stock.

6. Knorr vegetable stock tubs

The first oil is palm oil, followed by hydrolyzed yeast protein. It is a flavor cube, not a stock.

Swap: Kettle & Fire vegetable broth. A clean organic carton built from actual vegetables.

7. Miracle Whip

Not mayonnaise at all. Water, soybean oil and high fructose corn syrup built an empire as an impostor.

Swap: Primal Kitchen avocado oil mayo. Avocado oil, organic vinegar and cage-free eggs.

8. Rose's sweetened lime juice

The ingredients in order: water, then high fructose corn syrup. Lime comes after the sweetener.

Swap: Santa Cruz Organic Pure Lime Juice. The entire ingredient list is two words: organic lime juice.

9. Pepperidge Farm Farmhouse Hearty White

Marketed as the bread you would bake yourself, but the ingredient list says otherwise.

Swap: Silver Hills sprouted bread or Vermont Bread Company. Short lists, real whole grains.

10. Smucker's strawberry jam

Right after the strawberries come high fructose corn syrup and corn syrup.

Swap: Bonne Maman. Fruit, sugar, fruit pectin and lemon juice. A list short enough to read aloud.

11. Honey Nut Cheerios

Lists sugar, honey and brown sugar syrup as three separate sweeteners on the best-selling cereal in America.

Swap: Nature's Path Organic. The first certified organic breakfast brand, with no sugar stack.

12. Topo Chico

The cult favorite tested with among the highest PFAS levels of any bottled water tested by Consumer Reports.

Swap: San Pellegrino or Mountain Valley. Real spring sources with a mineral panel to show for it.
