The 12 grocery brands to never buy again

Each verdict below comes from reading the actual ingredient panel, not the front of the box. Next to every skip is the real swap. If we ever get one wrong, tell us and we will fix it publicly.

DRAFT — every entry below is a [VERIFY] placeholder. Each one must be replaced with a real brand and checked against the current ingredient label by a human before this page goes live. Nothing here ships as fact.

1. [VERIFY] Spreadable butter blend

Butter cut with canola or palm oil so it stays soft. It is a seed-oil spread wearing a butter label.

Swap: [VERIFY] any butter whose panel reads "cream, salt" and nothing else.

2. [VERIFY] "Maple" pancake syrup

Corn syrup, caramel color, and flavoring. There is no maple in it.

Swap: [VERIFY] 100% pure maple syrup, one ingredient.

3. [VERIFY] Shredded cheese with cellulose

Anti-caking starch and cellulose powder coat every shred. It also melts worse.

Swap: [VERIFY] a block of real cheese, grate it at home.

4. [VERIFY] Fruit-on-the-bottom yogurt

More added sugar per cup than some desserts, plus gums and "natural flavor."

Swap: [VERIFY] plain whole-milk yogurt, add real fruit.

5. [VERIFY] "Wheat" bread that is white bread dyed brown

First ingredient is refined flour; the brown comes from caramel color or molasses.

Swap: [VERIFY] a loaf where "whole wheat flour" is ingredient one and the list stays under 7 items.

6. [VERIFY] Deli meat with "celery powder"

"Uncured, no nitrates added" then cured with celery powder, which is nitrate under another name.

Swap: [VERIFY] sliced-to-order roast from the counter, short panel.

7. [VERIFY] "Olive oil" blend

Front says olive; the panel says mostly canola or soybean oil with olive oil last.

Swap: [VERIFY] extra virgin olive oil, single origin, dark bottle.

8. [VERIFY] Flavored instant oatmeal packets

Sugar is the second ingredient, plus flavoring. You pay triple for the packet.

Swap: [VERIFY] plain rolled oats, add your own cinnamon and fruit.

9. [VERIFY] "Cheese product" singles

Legally not allowed to be called cheese. The word "product" on a dairy label is the tell.

Swap: [VERIFY] real sliced cheddar or American made from actual cheese.

10. [VERIFY] Bottled "juice cocktail"

"Cocktail," "drink," or "beverage" after a fruit name means mostly water and corn syrup.

Swap: [VERIFY] 100% juice, or the whole fruit for less money.

11. [VERIFY] Granola bars marketed as healthy

Candy-bar sugar numbers with seed oil as the binder. Read it next to an actual candy bar sometime.

Swap: [VERIFY] nuts and a piece of fruit, or a bar with a 4-5 item panel.

12. [VERIFY] Canned soup with a chemistry-set label

Modified starch, MSG under cover names, and more sodium per can than a day needs.

Swap: [VERIFY] a short-panel broth base, add your own vegetables.
